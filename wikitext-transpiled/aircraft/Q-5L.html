Description[edit | edit source]

The Q-5L (NATO: Fantan; a type of gambling in southern China) is a rank VII Chinese strike aircraft with a battle rating of 10.7 (AB/SB) and 9.7 (RB). It was introduced in Update "Fire and Ice".

One of the latest revision of Q-5 series in PLAAF service and currently the "latest" manned fixed-wing jet in game; thanks to the revision on the avionics and the eventual installation of new sensors for ground attacks, as well as new precision bombs in service, the new Q-5L will prove deadly to ground targets with its lesser-to-counterparts yet suffice payload.

For players who have flown Q-5 series, Q-5L still retains the same flight performance- although shared many parts of a J-6A, due to the lack of weight-reduction measures, Q-5s are in fact structurally overweight; while it has better sustained turn to heavier jets such as Phantoms thanks to its engines, do note that the overweight issue still haunts the jet. Beware of any targets who has higher maneuverability, although your higher speed might save you from subsonic jets by flying away from the battlefield, any supersonic jets will have no problem chasing off Q-5L; another note is that Q-5L still retains the same control surfaces lock at transonic speeds (> 850 km/h and before hitting Mach 1.0), use the airbrakes according to the situation for both regaining control or "snap-shots" on enemy aerial targets.

Like all its predecessors in-game, Q-5L shares the very same protection. Due to the needs for ground attack and some protection for pilot, the cockpit is protected by steel plates around and comes with a bulletproof glass at the front of the canopy; while this might not be sufficient against any autocannons at its tier, it could at least make sure the pilot will not get knock-down right away by incoming shots or shrapnel. The engines are still unprotected though, but the installation of fire suppression system will make sure it will have greater odds to survive an fire onboard.

Pros and cons[edit | edit source]

Pros:

  • New HUD with ballistic computer
  • Built-in TVD camera for target acquisition; alternatively equipped with an advanced targeting pod
  • Access to 250/500 kg smart-bombs
  • Sufficient maneuverability at around 700 km/h
  • Great acceleration thanks to the WP-6-IIIA engines
  • Access to RWR

Cons:

  • Same bulky airframe as all its predecessors and still locks up at transonic speeds
  • The built-in camera only has 1.5x magnification
  • Lacks any type of AAM and countermeasures, unlike its technological predecessor A-5C
  • Type 23-2K cannons needs extra practice against aerial targets
  • Subpar payload to its NATO/Warsaw-Pact counterparts
